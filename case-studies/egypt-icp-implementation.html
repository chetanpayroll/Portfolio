Executive Summary

A US-based software company was opening a sales hub in Cairo and needed to hire 15 employees immediately. They had no legal entity and no understanding of Egyptian labor laws. I facilitated the setup through a local In-Country Partner (ICP), managing the entire registration and configuration process to meet a strict hiring deadline.

The Challenge

Egypt's payroll landscape is notoriously bureaucratic:

  • Social Insurance Complexity: Highly complex calculation brackets for basic vs. variable salary ("fixed" vs. "variable" elements).
  • Language Barrier: Most government forms are exclusively in Arabic.
  • Banking Strictness: Strict requirements for bank file formats to process salary transfers.

The Solution

I managed the project by bridging the gap between the US HQ and local authorities:

  1. ICP Selection: Vetted and selected a qualified local payroll provider (ICP) with English-speaking support.
  2. Salary Structure Design: Restructured the US-style offer letters (Total Cost) into compliant Egyptian contracts separating Basic Salary, Social Insurance, and Allowances to optimize tax efficiency.
  3. Gross-Up Calculator: Built a custom Excel calculator for the US HR team to accurately forecast total employment costs including employer social security contributions.
